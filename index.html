GovHack New Zealand • 2020

Participant • Getting Around Challenge Runner Up

During the weekend hackathon, our team developed and demoed a tool
that would help communities use open source and community data to help with improvements we called LivCity.
LivCity is an application that allows a city to gain community sourced information on selected areas.
This app functions by an administrator or researcher selecting an area to question,
this could be for example regarding air pollution, traffic, safety, waste and more.
Using feedback from the community, the appropriate authorities can develop needed infrastructure in
areas or appropriate social services, greatly improving the efficiency, usefulness,
and bringing innovation to the community.

GovHack New Zealand • 2019

Participant • Innovate New Zealand Award • Best use of data in a Civil Emergency Award

For GovHack 2019 the team created a tourism app designed to help connect visitors to local businesses
and help guide tourists to safe areas in the event of a civil emergency.
Localé is a tourism based application designed to better connect visitors to local businesses and events in a safe and stress free manner.
Localé will also include local area safety and evacuation information to improve community safety.
Data generated can be fed back to the council to aid in council and civil defense planning.
The end goal of our idea is to have a well designed app with a great user experience,
while accomplishing our core concepts well.
